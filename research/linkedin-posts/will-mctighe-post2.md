## Post Content

The best content triggers 1 of 2 reactions:
"That's so true!" or "How did I not know this?!"

Average content is skimmed, half-read, and forgotten before the reader
even hits "see more."

Here are 8 things you can do to fix that:

1/ Lead with authority
Attach your idea to a real experience, a specific name, or a proven result
in the first two lines.
"I've spoken to 50 recruiters" or "I studied 100 reports."

2/ Have one objective
Don't try to hire, sell, and build trust in the same post.
- Educational post: proof → problem → solution
- Trust post: story → lesson → application
- Conversion post: use a case study

3/ Lead with your POV
Write the whole thing first.
Then find your most unique — sometimes most contrarian — perspective
and move that to the top.

4/ Use fewer words
Cut the last 2 lines of every draft.
If the post still makes sense without them, they were never needed.

5/ Be very concise
Write the way you'd text a friend.
Read every post out loud before publishing.
If you wouldn't say it out loud, remove or replace it.

6/ Repeat your key points
Say your key point early and close on it one more time towards the end.
Most people say it once and assume it landed. It rarely does.

7/ Share real CTAs
Give people one clear ask they can do in 5 minutes.
Vague asks get ignored.

8/ Write titles with promise
"5 LinkedIn Tips" promises nothing.
"How to sell anything to anyone" promises an outcome.
Try: [How to] + [specific action] + [the outcome they actually want]

## Why This Post Was Selected

Will breaks down exactly why most B2B LinkedIn content gets ignored and provides 8 concrete fixes. The framework covering authority, single objective, POV-first structure, and conciseness is directly applicable to SaaS founders who default to corporate language and multi-purpose posts. The insight about having one objective per post — and matching structure to that objective — is one of the most actionable writing principles in B2B LinkedIn content and addresses the most common failure mode in founder-led content programs.
