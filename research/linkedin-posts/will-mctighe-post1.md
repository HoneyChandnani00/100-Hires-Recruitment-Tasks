## Post Content

Going viral on LinkedIn isn't complicated.
There's a system that anyone can follow.

1/ Identify top performers
Pull the top-performing posts across different niches.
Look at the topic, the hook, and the visual used.
Watch smaller creators too — they're usually first to find what works.

2/ Adapt to your niche
Adjust top performing posts to be interesting, helpful, and actionable.
Ask: Will your audience care about this topic?
Does the first line spark surprise, curiosity, or fear?
Is the main point something they can act on?

3/ Use high-quality visuals
A strong post with a weak picture dies before anyone reads the first line.

4/ Write what you know
Pick one topic and go deep enough to own it.
Give away your best insights for free.
Make every post something a reader can act on the same day.

5/ Write for LinkedIn
Open with proof in the first two or three lines: a name, a date, or a number.
Write the way you'd text a friend, with zero corporate jargon.
Keep the whole post between 1,250 and 3,000 characters.

6/ Post 3 types of content each week
- Growth posts: will go viral
- Trust posts: make sure they stay
- Conversion posts: drive them to buy

7/ Track everything
Once a month, pull up your top posts.
Look for patterns in topics, formats, and visual style.

8/ Repurpose your winners
Take your biggest winners and run them again 4-6 weeks later.
Tweak with new information or rebuild in a different format.
Your best ideas need more than one airing to stick.

## Why This Post Was Selected

Will presents a complete 8-step repeatable system for LinkedIn content — from research and creation to tracking and repurposing.
