## Post Content

I am just so tired of all the advice about LinkedIn and the new algorithm.
Everyone seems to contradict each other and they all have special jargon.

So here is a very unscientific but actionable list of what I have been
doing lately that has definitely been working really well for me.

1. I do text posts.
2. I write about the things I want people to hire me to do.
3. I write in plain English.
4. Every post needs to be useful on its own.
5. Especially for my target reader.
6. Ideally, useful enough to save.

And that is literally how I do my own posts.

There's TOO MUCH INFORMATION about LinkedIn. It's overwhelming.
The most likely outcome of consuming all the info is that you feel really
confused and just stall out.

LinkedIn is where I get most of my business and also where I've met some
really awesome people.

My approach probably doesn't work for everyone.
But it definitely works 10000% better than overloading yourself with best
practice and then not posting because you don't even know where to start.

If you're feeling stuck, maybe put down the reports and just start writing
about what you know, in conversational terms, in a way that's helpful for
other people.
