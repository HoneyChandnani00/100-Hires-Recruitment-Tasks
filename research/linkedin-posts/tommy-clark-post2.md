## Post Content

I take 5-10 calls per week with B2B founders who want to post more on LinkedIn.
About 50% of them have tried working with a content agency before.
It didn't go well.

If you're going to work with a content agency on your personal brand,
run them through this gauntlet:

(1) Ask who will actually be writing for your account.
If the agency is oddly low-priced, you're probably getting passed off to a
junior writer. Get the name of the person owning your account. Meet them.

(2) See if the founder is obsessed with content.
It's in your best interest to work with an agency whose leadership loves
the craft of writing and communications.

(3) Observe whether the agency is qualifying you.
Savvy agencies know which clients they're best equipped to support.
They aren't afraid to turn folks away.

(4) Understand how they adapt to your tone of voice.
Plenty of people can ghostwrite LinkedIn content based on generic best practices.
But they tend to lack the ability to adapt away from LinkedIn "broetry"
and formulaic hooks. Founders are particular about their tone. Rightfully so.

(5) Make sure you like each other as people.
For a founder-led content engagement to work, you'll need to sit down with
these folks on a biweekly basis and communicate over Slack almost daily.
Lack of vibe creates friction. That friction ruins the content creation workflow.
