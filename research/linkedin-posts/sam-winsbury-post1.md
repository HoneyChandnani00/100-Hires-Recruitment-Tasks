## Post Content

I'm 27. Done $3.9M+ in revenue across a couple businesses.
Not because I'm some business genius.
I've made loads of mistakes.

But there's ONE thing I got right:
I built my personal brand on LinkedIn.

And honestly? I attribute most of that $3.9M to this.
Because selling is 10x easier when more people know, like and trust you.

That's the bit most people miss.

They think growth comes from:
→ Sending more cold emails
→ Spending more on ads
→ Hiring more salespeople
→ Chasing more referrals
→ Hoping their "work speaks for itself"

Sure, some of those can help.
But in 2026, your work does NOT speak for itself.
You have to do the talking for your work.

Because someone in your industry is equally as good as you (maybe worse)
But they're more visible.

So they get:
→ The inbound leads
→ The keynote invites
→ The podcast features
→ The high-value clients
→ The partnerships
→ The opportunities

Not because they're better.
Because they're better known.
