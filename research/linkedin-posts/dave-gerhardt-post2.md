## Post Content

B2B Social: How ClickUp Cracked The Code By Taking It Seriously.

When I was thinking back to my conversation with Chris Cunningham at ClickUp
about their success with social, I realized that was the big secret.

Simple, not easy. They went for it.

They developed a real POV about what they should be doing on social and
they went and did it — vs. just doing the "checkbox marketing" version of
social and wondering why it's not working.

Most B2B companies are still underinvesting in organic social.
It's hard to measure, the ROI takes a while to show up, and for years it
was something you handed to whoever was junior on the team while the "real"
budget went to ads and events.

But all the AI stuff makes social a more important channel than ever.
You're either going to get your information from the LLMs or from social media.
Social media allows your content to spread and reach more of your ICP.

In a world where AI is eating everything, social might be one of the last
great channels where marketers can be placing confident bets.

Chris Cunningham is one of the founding members of the team at ClickUp and
now his full-time job is running social media — and they do it like an agency,
not like a software company.

You're probably not going to copy this whole playbook.
You don't need to. The point is to see what's possible, then pull the pieces
that make sense for your team.
